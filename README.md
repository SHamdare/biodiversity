# 🌿 Biodiversity Analysis Project

## 📌 Project Overview
This project explores biodiversity data from multiple national parks to understand species distribution, conservation status, and observation patterns. The analysis combines species information with recorded observations to identify trends and highlight conservation priorities.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze species distribution across different categories  
- Examine conservation status of species  
- Explore observation patterns across national parks  
- Identify relationships between species type and conservation risk  

---

## 📂 Datasets
Two datasets were used in this project:

- **species_info.csv**
  - Contains species names, categories, and conservation status  

- **observations.csv**
  - Contains recorded sightings of species across national parks over 7 days  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Python  
- Pandas  
- Seaborn  
- Matplotlib  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

## 🔍 Data Exploration
Initial exploration included:
- Inspecting dataset structure and data types  
- Handling missing values in conservation status  
- Summarizing species categories and counts  
- Merging datasets for combined analysis  

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- The majority of species are not listed under conservation concern, indicating a relatively stable ecosystem overall  
- Certain categories, particularly **mammals and birds**, show a higher proportion of protected or at-risk species  
- Conservation status is unevenly distributed across categories, suggesting that some types of species are more vulnerable than others  
- Observation data reveals variation across national parks, with some parks reporting significantly higher numbers of sightings  
- Differences in observation counts may reflect biodiversity levels, park size, or observation effort  

---

## 📈 Visualisations
The project includes:
- Distribution of conservation status  
- Species category vs conservation status  
- Observations across national parks  
- Comparative analysis of species sightings  

---

## ⚠️ Limitations
- Missing conservation status data required assumptions during cleaning  
- Observations are limited to a short time period (7 days)  
- External environmental factors and seasonal variations are not included  
- Observation counts may be influenced by effort rather than actual biodiversity  

---

## ✅ Conclusion
This analysis provides valuable insights into biodiversity and conservation patterns across national parks. While most species are not currently at risk, certain groups—particularly mammals and birds—require closer monitoring due to higher proportions of protected statuses.

The variation in observations across parks suggests differences in biodiversity or data collection efforts. Overall, the findings highlight the importance of continued monitoring and targeted conservation strategies to protect vulnerable species.

This project demonstrates how data analysis can be used to support environmental awareness and inform conservation decisions.
